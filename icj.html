The legal dispute regarding whether Ongwen, a war crime perpetrator, should be tried by Uganda’s own domestic court of the ICD or by the ICC
(ICC Prosecutor v. Dominic Ongwen)

Mr. Ongwen's case was brought to the ICC to try him as a Brigade Commander of the Sinia Brigade of the
Lord’s Resistance Army (LRA). Mr. Ongwen was accused of being the supreme commander of a terrorist group.
In this mock trial, Mr. Ongwen was found not guilty of committing war crimes but the ICC was given
jurisdiction over the case.

Allegations of Genocide under the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide (Ukraine v. Russian Federation)

The ongoing Russo-Ukraine conflict was brought to the ICJ to determine whether Russian attacks on Ukraine are
considered acts of genocide. Though the issue is still ongoing, the ICJ was given jurisdiction over the case
and Russia was found guilty on the grounds of genocide in this trial.

Maritime Dispute (Peru v. Chile)

Peru brought the maritime boundary dispute to the ICJ. Peru challenged the established maritime boundary and
fought for the equidistance principle to be applied. This mock trial ended with a victory for the respondants,
as the ICJ was found to have jurisdiction over the case, but the status quo on the maritime boundary remains.
